**Overview**

ClickUp's visual identity is built around productive density: many features, many teams, many workflows, all made to feel navigable through rounded containers, short high-confidence copy, and strong visual grouping. The brand pairs a sober black primary action system with the highly recognizable ClickUp gradient marks: purple-to-blue for the lower chevron and pink-to-orange for the upper chevron. The result is not playful in a toy-like way; it is energetic, maximal, and operational.

The marketing site has three connected modes. The main homepage mode is broad and cinematic, using giant Plus Jakarta Sans headlines, app-preview panels, customer proof, and dense grids of product chips. The AI/product mode adds brighter purples, pinks, cyan highlights, agent illustrations, and a sense of "software with teammates inside it." The docs/help mode is much plainer and search-led, using a utility surface, category cards, and system-font fallback when the protected Help Center blocks full asset access.

Key Characteristics:

- Use {colors.primary} as the dominant CTA and text anchor rather than a saturated brand color.
- Preserve the ClickUp logo gradient as a signature accent, especially {colors.logo-purple}, {colors.logo-blue}, {colors.logo-pink}, and {colors.logo-orange}.
- Build sections from rounded white panels on {colors.surface}, with thin grey borders and minimal elevation.
- Use very large, tightly tracked Plus Jakarta Sans for marketing claims.
- Use Inter for explanatory copy, button labels, nav links, and compact UI details.
- Add mono labels through {typography.label} for product taxonomy, feature groups, and app-chip rhythm.
- Make product breadth visible through chip walls, tab clusters, feature grids, and "replaces" lists.

**Colors**

Primary & Action

- {colors.primary} is the main action color. It appears on primary buttons, high-contrast CTA blocks, active nav controls, and UI silhouettes where ClickUp wants seriousness instead of color noise.
- {colors.accent} is the main purple signal. It supports highlighted pricing states, AI-related accents, selected UI outlines, and branded emphasis without replacing black as the core CTA.
- {colors.accentHover} and {colors.accentPressed} are the tighter interaction range for purple actions. Use them for hover, selected tabs, active chips, and focused product callouts.
- {colors.link} is the clean blue link/action color. It appears in the logo gradient, inline links, product highlights, and lightweight "learn more" moments.

Surfaces

- {colors.canvas} is the dominant page and card surface. ClickUp keeps much of the site white so the product screenshots, chips, and gradient accents can carry visual energy.
- {colors.surface} is the soft page background for bands, app boxes, pricing comparison areas, and search-led modules. It gives white cards a little room to breathe.
- {colors.surfaceAlt} is a pale lavender-grey utility surface. It works well for inactive tabs, secondary buttons, footer-adjacent panels, and dense product-control areas.
- {colors.deep-panel} and {colors.dark-footer} support dark proof, footer, and AI showcase moments. Use them sparingly; ClickUp is not a dark-first brand.

Neutrals & Text

- {colors.ink} is the older heading anchor used in classic marketing typography. It is slightly cooler and deeper than the button black.
- {colors.body} is the newer UI text and logo wordmark black. Use it for headings, primary labels, cards, pricing amounts, and navigation.
- {colors.muted} is the core secondary text color. It should carry descriptions, disclaimers, legal notes, and supporting text in cards.
- {colors.border} and {colors.borderStrong} define the light panel architecture. Borders often do more work than shadows in this system.

Semantic

- {colors.success} supports positive metrics, checks, status indicators, and ROI proof.
- {colors.warning} is a warm highlight for badges, callouts, or warning-state accents, not a primary brand field.
- {colors.error} is reserved for destructive/error states and occasional red product illustrations.

Brand-specific Signatures

- {colors.logo-purple}, {colors.logo-blue}, {colors.logo-pink}, and {colors.logo-orange} are the ClickUp mark colors. They are most recognizable when paired as diagonal gradients rather than isolated swatches.
- {colors.ai-pink} and {colors.ai-cyan} identify AI, Brain, and Super Agent moments. Use them for energetic badges, shimmer text, and agent panels.
- {colors.product-teal}, {colors.product-yellow}, and {colors.product-violet-soft} appear in product illustrations, app icons, and workflow tiles.
- {colors.pale-purple}, {colors.pale-pink}, {colors.pale-blue}, and {colors.pale-orange} are the safe way to bring brand color into large surfaces.

Accent families: Sometype Mono for product taxonomy labels; Shantell Sans for occasional handwritten annotation labels observed in the v4 system.

Principles:

- Track big headlines negatively; ClickUp display type should feel compressed, confident, and billboard-like.
- Keep body copy lighter and quieter than headings; Inter descriptions should create breathing room around dense feature counts.
- Use heavy button text. Primary actions are compact but assertive, with an 800-ish weight and rounded button geometry.
- Use mono labels to organize complexity. The mono style makes categories feel like product modules rather than editorial captions.
- Avoid long, lyrical paragraphs in hero sections. ClickUp's typographic voice is short, declarative, and benefit-stacked.

**Layout**

The core desktop container is roughly 1120-1160px, with wide marketing sections that often extend visual media beyond text columns. Hero sections center-align the major claim, then quickly move into large product previews, chip rows, or grid modules. Use {spacing.section} for major vertical rhythm on desktop and reduce to {spacing.xxl} on mobile.

Spacing scale: {spacing.xs} for icon gaps, {spacing.sm} for tab interiors, {spacing.md} for chip padding and dense card gutters, {spacing.lg} for button and card content rhythm, {spacing.xl} for component groups, {spacing.xxl} for mobile sections, and {spacing.section} for full desktop bands.

Grid behavior favors modular density. Feature sections use 2-4 column grids, pricing uses side-by-side cards that collapse vertically, and product breadth is expressed through wrapped app chips rather than sparse single-message panels.

Whitespace philosophy: ClickUp does not use empty luxury whitespace as its main brand cue. It uses enough whitespace to make a very large catalog feel digestible, then fills the system with cards, tabs, logos, chips, badges, and UI previews.

**Elevation & Depth**

ClickUp is mostly flat and card-based. The standard marketing panel uses {components.hero-card} with a soft `0 4px 10px rgba(13,21,48,0.05)` shadow, while smaller cards use compact overlays like `0 1px 3px rgba(0,0,0,0.10)`.

Depth is created by surface contrast, rounded corners, nested UI previews, and borders more than by dramatic shadows. Pricing and feature cards often combine a pale border with a tiny overlay shadow so the page stays crisp at high content density.

The strongest depth appears in highlighted pricing cards and agent/product media. These can use inset purple outlines, pale inner glows, or `0 10px 25px rgba(18,43,165,0.10)`, but avoid glassmorphism unless reproducing an observed navigation or AI showcase element.

**Components**

Buttons

{components.button-primary} is the workhorse CTA. It is black, rounded to {rounded.xl}, compact, and high contrast; use it for "Get started," signup, demo, and pricing conversion.

{components.button-primary-hover} stays dark rather than turning colorful. The interaction should feel like a darkening or slight press, not a chromatic state change.

{components.button-secondary} carries secondary actions with pale lavender-grey fill and purple text. It is useful near primary CTAs when ClickUp needs a gentler "Learn more" or category action.

{components.button-tertiary} is a lightweight utility action. It belongs in nav menus, card controls, Help Center categories, and subtle inline interactions.

{components.button-ai-pink} is specific to Brain, AI, and agent modules. Use it for moments that need a deliberate AI flavor without turning the entire page pink.

Cards & Containers

{components.hero-card} is the large rounded product-preview container. It often holds screenshots, video placeholders, or multi-pane app demonstrations on a clean surface.

{components.feature-card} is the standard modular marketing card. It should contain one feature idea, one compact headline, and a small visual or icon rather than broad editorial copy.

{components.agent-card} is the ClickUp AI/Super Agent card pattern. It can use pale purple, pastel character accents, small capability labels, and action snippets such as update task, create event, send email, or complete task.

{components.app-chip} is central to the brand's "everything app" story. Use many chips in rows or wrapped clouds to show breadth: Projects, Chat, Brain, Calendar, Docs, Whiteboards, Dashboards, Automations, and related modules.

Inputs & Forms

{components.input-search} fits the Help Center and docs mode. It should be large enough to feel like a command/search bar, with a white fill, rounded corners, and calm grey border.

Form fields should remain utility-first. Use {colors.canvas}, {colors.border}, and {colors.body}; reserve colorful borders for focus or AI-assisted states.

Navigation

{components.nav-bar} is compact and rounded, with a ClickUp logo lockup, top-level product groupings, pricing/enterprise links, and right-aligned demo/login/signup actions. It should feel dense but not cramped.

Navigation states are restrained. Use black text for active/high-confidence choices, {colors.muted} for secondary links, and small chevrons or icon sprites instead of heavy underline treatments.

Footer

{components.footer} is a dense trust and sitemap module. It uses a dark or near-black base, column lists, compliance badges, social icons, and low-contrast legal links.

Pricing

{components.pricing-card} is a white rounded plan panel with subtle elevation. It should make plan names, prices, CTAs, and feature bullets scannable at high density.

{components.pricing-card-highlight} marks the recommended or emphasized plan. Use the purple outline and inner pale highlight, but do not flood the whole plan card with saturated purple.

Pricing tables should use compact typography, checkmarks, pale dividers, and sticky comparison logic if the page is long. Keep the conversion CTA visible near the plan title.

Signature Components

The replacement-app chip wall is a signature ClickUp product pattern. It visually proves breadth by naming dozens of modules in a compact grid, often around a headline about replacing fragmented software.

The Super Agent module is another signature pattern. It combines illustrated agent characters, action labels, 24/7/infinite-memory claims, and UI-like task snippets to make AI feel operational.

The Brain chat card pattern shows conversational work inside the product. It should look like a real workspace message, not a generic chatbot bubble.

The proof stack combines ROI metrics, G2 awards, customer cards, and logos. Keep these modules dense and credible, with strong numeric hierarchy and restrained brand color.

**Do's and Don'ts**

Do's:

- Use {colors.primary} for the main CTA even when a page contains bright gradient artwork.
- Pair large {typography.hero} or {typography.headline-lg} text with compact Inter body copy to preserve ClickUp's confident hierarchy.
- Wrap product breadth in {components.app-chip} rows instead of hiding it in paragraphs.
- Use {rounded.xl}, {rounded.xxl}, and {rounded.section} generously on cards and media containers.
- Use {colors.surface} behind white cards when a page needs dense grids or pricing comparisons.
- Add ClickUp signature accents through the logo gradient colors, not random rainbow palettes.
- Use {typography.label} for feature taxonomy, team labels, and product category rows.
- Keep pricing emphasis as a border/inset highlight through {components.pricing-card-highlight}.

Don'ts:

- Do not make purple the default primary CTA; ClickUp's observed conversion buttons are mostly {colors.primary}.
- Do not use sharp-cornered enterprise SaaS cards; the system needs rounded panel geometry.
- Do not invent heavy neon glows or glass panels for normal marketing sections.
- Do not replace the chip wall with a sparse three-card feature list when expressing product breadth.
- Do not center long paragraphs under the hero; use short bullets and compact benefit stacks.
- Do not use low-contrast grey text below {colors.muted} for important pricing or feature claims.
- Do not mix decorative gradients into every card; reserve gradient energy for logo, AI, and hero media accents.
- Do not use generic system typography for marketing pages unless rendering a Help Center fallback.

**Responsive Behavior**

| Breakpoint | Width | Behavior |
|---|---:|---|
| mobile-sm | 360px | Single column, full-width CTAs, compact chips |
| mobile | 600px | Hide large banner extras, stack cards, reduce hero type |
| tablet | 768px | Two-column cards where space allows, collapsed nav |
| laptop | 1024px | Standard content container, multi-column feature grids |
| desktop | 1200px | Full nav, wide hero media, pricing cards side by side |
| wide | 1440px | Preserve 1120-1160px content max; extend background art only |

Touch targets should be at least 44px high for primary CTAs, nav menu items, pricing buttons, and Help Center search actions. Chips can be visually smaller, but interactive chips should still have enough vertical padding to tap cleanly.

Collapsing strategy:

- Collapse global navigation into a menu before links wrap.
- Stack pricing cards vertically and keep each plan CTA immediately below the price.
- Convert large product preview composites into a single dominant image or carousel-like stack.
- Let app chips wrap naturally rather than shrinking text below readable sizes.
- Reduce {spacing.section} to {spacing.xxl} and reduce display type by at least one token level.

Image behavior: product screenshots should scale fluidly inside rounded containers and preserve their UI detail. Decorative agent illustrations may crop more aggressively, but never crop the face/body so much that the AI teammate metaphor disappears.

**Iteration Guide**

1. Check the primary CTA: it should use {components.button-primary}, not a generic blue or purple button.
2. Check display type: hero and section headlines should use Plus Jakarta Sans, tight tracking, and one of {typography.hero}, {typography.headline-lg}, or {typography.headline-md}.
3. Check density: if a ClickUp marketing screen only has three sparse cards, add app chips, proof metrics, tabs, or product modules.
4. Check surfaces: white cards should sit on {colors.canvas} or {colors.surface}, with borders from {colors.border} and only subtle shadows.
5. Check brand color: gradient accents should map back to {colors.logo-purple}, {colors.logo-blue}, {colors.logo-pink}, and {colors.logo-orange}.
6. Check AI modules: Brain or Agent sections should include {colors.ai-pink}, {colors.ai-cyan}, agent cards, chat/task snippets, or action labels.
7. Check pricing: recommended plans should use {components.pricing-card-highlight}, not a fully saturated purple panel.
8. Check mobile: nav should collapse, cards should stack, and CTAs should remain at least 44px tall.
